Before We Build Anything
The tools don't matter.
The outcome does.
  • Tools change. Bland today, something else in 6 months. That's fine.
  • Technical paralysis is real. Don't let "which tool is best" stop you from building anything.
  • Hype is louder than signal right now. Ignore 90% of it.
  • The question is never "which AI phone tool should I use?" – it's "what should my COS own on the phone?"
Let Your COS Guide the Build

Tell your COS the outcome you want. Push back on requirements. Ask it to simplify. It will tell you what you actually need.

When someone asks "X vs Y"

Ask yourself: does it accomplish the outcome? Is it the simplest path? If yes – build it. You can swap the tool later.

Infrastructure Tip
Tailscale – Access Your COS Anywhere

Your COS runs on your machine. Tailscale gives you a private, encrypted tunnel back to it – from your phone, a hotel room, anywhere.

  • Access your files and system remotely without exposing anything to the public internet
  • Share screen or control your Mac from anywhere once it's set up
  • Essential once you start building automations that run 24/7
  • Free for personal use, works across Mac, iPhone, Windows
tailscale.com →
Setup Order
  1. 1. Install Tailscale on your Mac
  2. 2. Install on your iPhone
  3. 3. Sign in with the same account on both
  4. 4. Enable SSH on your Mac (System Settings → General → Sharing)
⚠ Permissions Disclaimer

Once Tailscale is installed, you must grant Full Disk Access and Screen Recording permissions in System Settings → Privacy & Security – otherwise remote access to files and screen share will not work.

Decision Framework
What Your COS Owns vs. What It Escalates
COS Owns
  • Cold or unknown callers
  • Scheduling requests
  • Status updates ("is X done?")
  • Vendor follow-ups
  • Intake and triage
Escalate to You
  • Existing clients with urgent issues
  • Anyone you've flagged as VIP
  • Emotional or sensitive conversations
  • Decisions only you can make
