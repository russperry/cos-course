Step-by-Step Build Guide

Follow along during the session. Every command is copy-paste ready. You don't need to memorize anything — just run what's here.

Step 1 — Install Ghostty Terminal Tool Setup

Ghostty is your terminal — the window into your COS. If that word scares you, good. It won't by the end of tonight.

1. Go to ghostty.org and download for your OS.
2. Install it like any other app.
3. Open it. You'll see a command prompt. That's it. You're in.

What is a terminal? It's just a text-based way to talk to your computer. Instead of clicking buttons, you type instructions. Your COS lives here.

Step 2 — Install Hermes Agent Core Install

Hermes is the runtime — the operating system your AI COS lives inside. One command installs everything.

In Tabby, paste this and press Enter:

curl -fsSL https://hermes-agent.nousresearch.com/install.sh | sh

It'll run for about 60 seconds. When it finishes, type:

hermes --version

If you see a version number, you're good. That's your COS runtime installed.

Step 3 — Set Up Claude API Account AI Brain

Claude API is the brain. Pay-as-you-go — ~$10-20/mo to start. The only ongoing cost in this entire stack.

Go to platform.claude.com, create an account, add $10-20 in credits, and generate an API key.

Then in Ghostty:

hermes config set model.provider anthropic
hermes config set model.default claude-sonnet-4-5
hermes config set ANTHROPIC_API_KEY sk-ant...HERE

Test it:

hermes "Say hello and tell me you're my new Chief of Staff"

When it replies — that's your COS saying hello for the first time.

Step 4 — Write Your CONTEXT.md The Brain File

This is the most important file you'll ever write for your COS. It's what makes it yours — not a generic AI, but a system that knows your business, your voice, and your priorities.

Create the file:

mkdir -p ~/my-cos && nano ~/my-cos/CONTEXT.md

Paste this template and fill it in during the session:

# My Name — Context for My AI Chief of Staff

## Who I Am
[Your name, role, company, what you do in 2-3 sentences]

## My Business
[What you sell, who you sell it to, current focus / biggest priorities]

## My Voice
[How you write emails. Casual or formal? Short or long? Sign off with?]

## My Top 3 Priorities Right Now
1. [Priority 1]
2. [Priority 2]
3. [Priority 3]

## Tools I Use Daily
[Email, calendar, Slack, CRM — whatever matters]

Save with Ctrl+X, then Y, then Enter.

Now tell Hermes where to find it:

hermes config set context_file ~/my-cos/CONTEXT.md
Step 5 — Wire Gmail + Google Calendar First Integration

This is where it gets real. We're giving your COS access to your inbox and calendar — the two places where most of your work actually happens.

hermes setup google-workspace

This walks you through a one-time OAuth flow. You'll see a URL — open it in your browser, authorize, paste the code back. Takes about 3 minutes.

Test it:

hermes "How many unread emails do I have right now?"

When it answers correctly — your COS is inside your inbox.

Step 6 — Deploy Email Triage + Morning Brief Automation Live

The payoff. Your COS sorts your email into 3 tiers every 30 minutes, and sends you a morning summary to your phone. This alone is worth the entire course.

3-Tier Triage:

Tier 1 – Respond Now: Board, investors, direct reports with escalations, revenue-critical.
Tier 2 – Handle Today: Team leads, active projects, scheduling, customer feedback.
Tier 3 – FYI/Archive: Newsletters, notifications, cold outreach. 

hermes "Set up an automated email triage that runs every 30 minutes. Sort into 3 tiers: Tier 1 (respond now - board, investors, escalations), Tier 2 (handle today - team, projects, customers), Tier 3 (archive - newsletters, notifications). Label each email with its tier."

Morning Brief to your phone:

hermes "Set up a daily morning brief at 7 AM that summarizes: unread Tier 1 and Tier 2 emails, today's calendar, and my top 3 priorities from CONTEXT.md. Send it to [your-telegram-or-email]."

Tomorrow morning, your phone gets a brief before you've had your first cup of coffee. That's your COS working while you sleep.

Step 7 — Live Test: Your COS Sends an Email The Moment

The finale. We'll pick a real email from your inbox and have your COS draft and send a reply — live, in front of the class.

hermes "Find an email in my inbox that needs a reply. Draft a response in my voice, show it to me first, and when I say 'send' — send it."

When that email goes out — you've crossed the line. You're not just using AI. You have a Chief of Staff.
