The Real Definition
What Context Actually Means

Before any meeting, your COS should be able to answer four questions without you asking:

Who They Are

Role, company, how you met, where they sit in your world

Last Time You Talked

When, about what, what was promised, what was open

What They Care About

Priorities, sensitivities, what moves the needle for them

What You Want

The single outcome you need from this meeting

The Foundation
The Contacts System
  • One markdown file per person
  • Lives in ~/contacts/ on your machine
  • Your COS reads it before every interaction
  • Simple, portable, future-proof – just text files
  • You own it forever, no SaaS lock-in
~/contacts/ ~/contacts/ ├── sarah-chen.md ├── marcus-bailey.md ├── jenna-rodriguez.md ├── david-kim.md └── _index.md
Structure
What Goes In a Contact File
  • Name – full name + how they're addressed
  • Role + Company – what they do, where
  • Relationship notes – how you met, why they matter
  • Last interaction – when, what, what's open
  • What they care about – priorities, hot buttons
  • How they communicate – direct, formal, slow, fast
sarah-chen.md # Sarah Chen Role: VP Marketing, Northstar Met: SaaStr 2024 Cares about: ROI per channel, straight talk, no fluff Comms: short, direct, no emoji Last: Apr 22 – pitched Q3 retainer, she asked for case studies Open: send 2 case studies + pricing

Live Build · Part 2
The prep Command
  1. 1Reads your calendar for the next event
  2. 2Finds the matching contact file by name or email
  3. 3Generates the prep card from contact + meeting context
  4. 4Drops it on cron: runs 30 minutes before every event
One command. Runs forever. Every meeting gets a briefing.

Avoid These
Common Mistakes
Contact Files Too Long

If you can't scan it in 10 seconds, your COS is wasting tokens. Keep it tight – the goal is signal, not biography.

Prep Cards Too Generic

If the card could be about anyone, it's broken. Force it to use the actual contact file – names, history, specifics.

Not Testing It Live

Don't trust it until you've used it on a real meeting. Run it tonight, even on a low-stakes call.
