**FEED POST 6 — The Security Reality Check**

📸 *Visual: Mac + Tailscale logo + lock graphic*

**Caption:**
"Is it safe to run this on my main computer?"

Students asked this last night. Great question.

Short answer: running this locally is SAFER than running it in the cloud.

Cloud setups have more configuration surface area for mistakes. Local is contained.

Here's the basic security stack:

**1. Closed WiFi** — your home network requires a password. That's it.

**2. Mac Firewall** — System Settings → Network → Firewall. Built in. One toggle.

**3. Tailscale** — Free for 3 devices. Creates a private encrypted network between your phone, laptop, and home Mac. Nobody can reach your machine even if they found your IP.

The real question isn't "is this safe?" It's "am I comfortable with Claude having access to my Gmail?"

For most founders — yes. Your email, your calendar, your tasks. That's the point.

The risk of NOT building your own system is staying overwhelmed forever.

Here's how to use it:

1. Tell your AI what you want to build (email triage, morning brief, whatever)
2. Paste this prompt at the end
3. Answer the 5 questions honestly
4. Read the summary
5. Say "let's go"

It quizzes out the specifics you didn't think of. It catches what you forgot to mention. By question 4 you're like "oh yeah that too, add that."

By the end it builds something that actually fits how you work — not what it assumed you wanted.
